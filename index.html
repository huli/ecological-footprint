The
Ecological Footprint
of our world

The Ecological Footprint measures the demand on and supply of nature.

The metric tracks our demand of six categories of productive surface areas: cropland, grazing land, fishing grounds, built-up land, forest area, and carbon demand on land.

If the demand is bigger than the supply. The world, sooner or later will not be able to recover.

Let's see, how we do.

Human development drives footprint

The further a country has developed, the higher the footprint in most cases is. The development of a country is measured with the Human development Index (see below).

With a global footprint of 2.9 hectares we are using more than 1.5 times what our nature can regenerate. The world currently has a biocapacity of 1.7 hectares per capita - the biocapacity represents the productivity of its ecological assets.

Human Development Index (HDI) is a composite statistic of average achievement in key dimensions of human development: a long and healthy life, being knowledgeable and have a decent standard of living.

Who is doing best?

Haiti, Eritrea and Burundi are at the bottom of the chart. They are only using very little ressources and their footprints are the smallest of all the countries.

Haiti for example has only an ecological footprint of 0.6 hectares per capita and is therefore only using a third of the world populations mean.

Who is not doing well, at all

The countries at the top of the chart are using the most ressources per capita. Luxembourg builds the very top, followed by Canada and Australia.

Luxembourg has a footprint of enormous 13 hectares per capita and is therefore using more than 21 times the natural ressources of Haiti.

How has the global footprint
changed over time?

Our global footprint is constantly growing while the biocapacity of our planet is constantly decreasing.

Between 1961 and the year 2013 the footprint has grown from 2.3 to 2.9 hectares (PC) while the biocapacity has shrunk from 3.1 to worrying 1.7

Which country has made the biggest change for the better?

Cyprus, Greece and Barbados are the countries with the best tendencies in recent years.

While the footprint of Cyprus is still much higher than in other countries. It has reduced the consumption of ressources by a third since 2008.

Which countries made the biggest change for worse?

Timor-Leste, Djibouti and Fiji are the countries with the highest increaes in consumption.

While Fiji has its footprint grown by frightening 49 percent, the country today has still a smaller footprint that many others.
