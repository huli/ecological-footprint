The ecological footprint
of our world

The ecological footprint measures the amount of natural resources we use in one year.

If the demand we oppose to our nature exceeds the supply, the world will not be able to recover.

The resources of our planet are limited.

Let's see, how we do.

Human development drives footprint

The more a country has progressed with what is normally referred to as human development, the higher the footprint most often is.

The world currently has a biocapacity of 1.7 hectares per capita.

The Human Development Index (HDI) is a composite statistic of average achievement in key dimensions of human development: a long and healthy life, being knowledgeable and have a decent standard of living.

Who is doing well?

Haiti, Burundi and Timor-Leste are doing very well for example.

STORY MISSING
Haiti has a ecological footprint of 0.6 hectares per capita and is therefore only using a third of the world populations mean.
